<p><strong>Who are the best left-handed hitters in baseball history?</strong> Ted Williams, Babe Ruth, and Barry Bonds top nearly every list. Left-handed hitters hold a natural advantage — they stand closer to first base and see more right-handed pitching.</p><p>Here are nine of the greatest lefty hitters of all time.</p><h2 id="ted-williams">Ted Williams</h2><p>Theodore Samuel Williams was undoubtedly one of the best left handed hitters in history. From 1986 to 1992, he was a member of the Pittsburgh Pirates and from 1993 to 2007, he was with the San Francisco Giants. He's regarded as one of the game's all-time greats.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="/content/images/2022/01/barry-bonds.jpg" class="kg-image" alt="Barry Bonds, record-breaking left-handed power hitter" loading="lazy" width="276" height="400"><figcaption>Barry Bonds</figcaption></figure><h2 id="lou-gehrig">Lou Gehrig</h2><p>Louis Henry "Lou" Gehrig was an American baseball first baseman who played 17 seasons in Major League Baseball (MLB) for the New York Yankees, from 1923 through 1939.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="/content/images/2022/01/lou-gehrig.jpg" class="kg-image" alt="Lou Gehrig, left-handed first baseman for the New York Yankees" loading="lazy" width="331" height="400"><figcaption>Lou Gehrig</figcaption></figure><h2 id="stan-musial">Stan Musial</h2><p>Stanley Frank Musial, nicknamed "Stan the Man", was an American baseball outfielder and first baseman.
