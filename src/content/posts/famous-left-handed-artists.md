Escher are among the most celebrated left-handed artists in history.</p><p>Their left-handedness influenced distinctive techniques — da Vinci's mirror writing and cross-hatching, Escher's mastery of perspective — that helped define entire artistic movements.</p><h2 id="list-of-famous-left-handed-artists">List of famous left-handed artists</h2><ol><li><strong>Leonardo da Vinci</strong><br>Perhaps the most renowned left-handed artist, Leonardo da Vinci's iconic works such as the <em>Mona Lisa</em> and <em>The Last Supper. </em>Perhaps the most renowned left-handed artist, Leonardo da Vinci's iconic works, such as the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, remain central to art history. His left-handedness is believed to have influenced his unique cross-hatching technique and distinct mirror-writing style.</li><li><strong>Michelangelo</strong><br>Michelangelo, another luminary of the Renaissance, was also left-handed. His celebrated works, including the <em>Sistine Chapel ceiling</em> and the statue of <em>David</em>, demonstrate his expertise. His left-handedness may have influenced his approach to sculpting and painting.</li><li><strong>Rembrandt</strong><br>Known for his exceptional realism and empathy, Rembrandt, a left-hander, brought a unique sensibility to his paintings. His self-portraits and biblical scenes are prime examples of his artistry and skill.</li><li><strong>Raphael</strong><br>High Renaissance master Raphael is celebrated for his serene and harmonious paintings. Although less documented, his left-handedness likely contributed to his distinct artistic approach.</li><li><strong>Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec</strong><br>Using his left hand, this post-impressionist painter vividly captured the Parisian nightlife. His bold colors and candid portrayals were notable for their unique perspective and vibrancy.</li><li><strong>Paul Klee</strong><br>Paul Klee, a left-handed painter, was known for his distinctive abstract style, blending surrealism, cubism, and expressionism. His work demonstrates the versatility and innovation of left-handed artists.</li><li><strong>Marc Chagall</strong><br>Chagall's left-handedness brought a unique dimension to his work, celebrated for its dream-like quality and whimsical figures. His art seamlessly blended fantasy with traditional motifs.</li><li><strong>M.C. Escher</strong><br>Escher, renowned for his mind-bending optical illusions, utilized his left-handedness to enhance his approach to perspective and intricate detailing.
