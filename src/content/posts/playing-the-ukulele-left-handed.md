<p><strong>Can you play ukulele left-handed?</strong> Yes. Either buy a dedicated left-handed ukulele or restring a standard soprano ukulele so the low G string sits on the bottom and the high C string on top.</p>

<p>Hold the body on your right thigh with the neck pointing away, and use your right hand to fret chords.</p><h2 id="which-type-of-ukulele-is-right-for-you">Which type of ukulele is right for you?</h2><p>Start with a soprano. It's the standard size, the cheapest, and the easiest to learn on. Concert and tenor ukuleles are bigger, with a deeper sound — worth a look if your hands are large or you want more low end, but skip them as a first instrument.</p><h2 id="make-sure-its-a-left-handed-ukulele">Make sure it's a left-handed ukulele</h2><p>The strings need to run in reverse order: low G on the bottom, high C on top. Some shops sell lefty models off the shelf; otherwise any standard soprano can be restrung in five minutes. If you're buying online, say "left-handed" explicitly — a flipped photo isn't the same thing.</p><h2 id="how-to-hold-your-ukulele">How to hold your ukulele</h2><p>Body on your right thigh, neck pointing away from you, fretting hand on the right. That's the whole posture. It feels natural for lefties from the first minute — no breaking-in period.</p><h2 id="play-some-chords">Play some chords!</h2><p>Three chords get you through most campfire songs: C, F, G. Fingerings for a lefty setup:</p><p><strong>C chord:</strong> Index on the third fret of the low G string. Middle on the second fret of the C string. Ring on the first fret of the E string.</p><p><strong>F chord:</strong> Index on the first fret of the low G string. Middle on the second fret of the C string. Ring on the third fret of the E string.</p><p><strong>G chord:</strong> Index on the second fret of the low G string. Middle finger on the third fret of the E string. Ring finger on the third fret of the A string.</p><h2 id="next-steps">Next steps</h2><p>Once those three chords feel automatic, learn one strumming pattern (down, down-up, up-down-up) and you've got the engine for hundreds of songs. The rest is reps.</p>
